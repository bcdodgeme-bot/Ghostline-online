Frequently Asked Questions

What is a value proposition?

A value proposition for customers is a statement that effectively conveys the distinctive advantages and benefits of your product or service to your target audience. It not only emphasizes the specific issues that your product or service solves but also showcases how it outperforms your competitors. A well-crafted value proposition sets your business apart and draws in potential customers.

On the other hand, an employee value proposition is a statement that’s meant to attract potential employees to your organization. It communicates the unique set of benefits and offerings a prospective employee would receive in return for what they provide to the organization.

How long should a value proposition be?

A value proposition can follow many formats in terms of length, but ideally, it should be relatively concise—around two to three sentences. Ensure that each word clearly strengthens your selling point or else omit it.

Consider using Grammarly’s AI writing assistance to draft value proposition templates if you need help getting started.

What information should be considered when writing a value prop?

Learning how to write a value proposition and the relevant information to include involves distilling down what your company or business does better than anyone else or what sets you apart. Focus on the benefits you provide and stick to relevant facts instead of making bold claims. Consider emphasizing the following things in your value prop statement:  

- the product or service you are selling (or your organization itself, in the case of an employee value prop) 

- your target audience 

-how your product/service or organization improves the audience's life 

- reasons why the buyer or prospective talent should select your product/service or company instead of competitors 

- a timeline of when the value of your product/service will be delivered or completed (for customer value propositions)
