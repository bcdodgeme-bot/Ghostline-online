NIH Research Matters

August 5, 2025 Edition

  • Compounds may help treat certain neurological disorders

    An experimental small molecule alleviated CoQ10 deficiency and reversed associated brain damage in a boy with a rare disease.

  • Scientists create organoids with specialized blood vessels

  • Neurons can tap into fat for fuel

More Stories

  • Factors linked to lung cancer in never-smokers

    July 15, 2025 — A study linked fine-particle air pollution and other environmental factors to genetic changes in people with lung cancer who never smoked.

  • Engineering immune cells within the body

    July 15, 2025 — A new method to engineer T cells within living organisms could make CAR T cell therapies more accessible for treating cancer and autoimmune diseases.

  • Measuring aging with brain scans

    July 15, 2025 — Researchers developed a way to measure how fast a person is aging from a single brain scan.

  • Home test kits boost screening for cervical cancer

    July 1, 2025 — Women who used at-home test kits were more than twice as likely to complete screening for cervical cancer than women who received only telephone reminders. 

  • Education levels may affect mortality trends

    July 1, 2025 — Mortality rates in 2023 were higher than expected based on earlier trends, especially among those who did not graduate from college.

  • How exercise can protect against Alzheimer’s

    July 1, 2025 — Mouse studies give insights into how exercise might help protect against Alzheimer’s disease.

  • Y chromosome loss plays complex role in cancers

    June 17, 2025 — Loss of the Y chromosome in both tumor and immune cells may hamper immune protections and lead to poorer outcomes for men with different types of cancer.

  • Cysteine restriction plays key role in weight loss

    June 17, 2025 — Studies in people and mice show a diet that’s low in the amino acid cysteine reprograms fat to burn more calories, triggering weight loss and reduced inflammation.

  • Slowing malaria by treating mosquitoes

    June 17, 2025 — Researchers identified compounds that could kill malaria parasites inside mosquitoes.

  • Infant with rare disease receives customized gene therapy

    June 3, 2025 — Researchers used a personalized gene-editing approach to mend a faulty gene in a baby born with a rare, often deadly disorder.
