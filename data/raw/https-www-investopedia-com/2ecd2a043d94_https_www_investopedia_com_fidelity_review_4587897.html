Our Take

Brokerage industry heavyweight Fidelity continues to be our top broker for low costs and ETFs this year, and has also topped our rankings as the best broker for cash management for the first time.

Fidelity offers customers competitive pricing, nearly every account type, and a broad array of trading products, including stocks, ETFs, mutual funds, options, fixed income (U.S. Treasurys, corporates, municipal bonds, and CDs), some cryptocurrency trading options, and penny stocks (after receiving trading approval). There are also robo-trading and pre-packaged investment products available at Fidelity.

Beyond the trading products, Fidelity provides excellent desktop and mobile platforms, tons of calculators and portfolio management tools, excellent research and news, and vast educational content.

Editor's Note

In addition to our review of Fidelity's online brokerage platform, we've also reviewed the company's robo-advisor service, Fidelity Go.

Introduction

Fidelity was founded in Boston by Edward C. Johnson and is now one of the largest brokerage companies in the world, with $15.1 trillion in assets under administration, $5.9 trillion in discretionary assets, and 3.5 million daily average customer trades. Fidelity also manages employee retirement accounts for over 28,800 businesses and supports more than 15,700 wealth management firms and institutions. Fidelity has more than 77,000 employees and 216 investor centers in the U.S. The company remains family-owned and operated.

Fidelity continues to innovate and improve to better serve its customers. Some recent updates include launching the Fidelity Ethereum Fund (FETH), creating the Fidelity robo advisor to manage simplified employee pension plans (SEP IRAs), adding fractional share trading within Personalized Portfolios, and employing new technology to help small- to mid-sized investment advisors help grow their businesses. Fidelity also expanded Fidelity Medicare Services to all 50 states, launched a Student Debt program that companies use to help their employees pay off their student loans, and released its 15th annual Plan Sponsor Attitudes Study, which helps advisors better understand their clients.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Low costs and fees

  • Wide selection of products to trade

  • Excellent order execution

  • Great screeners

  • Free third-party research

Cons

  • Mobile app lacks punch for advanced users

  • No futures, options on futures, or spot forex

Pros Explained

  • Low costs and fees: Fidelity offers free online commission trading on stock and ETFs, as well as thousands of no fee mutual funds to choose from. Options pricing and margin rates are also competitive. Further, there are no fees for outgoing transfers and many other items that some brokers charge. 
  • Wide selection of products to trade: Stocks, ETFs, 10,000-plus mutual funds, as well as U.S. Treasury bonds, corporate bonds, municipal bonds, CDs, international stocks and ETFs in 25 countries across 16 currencies, basket portfolios, physical metals including gold bars and coins, and three popular cryptocurrencies are available for trading at Fidelity.
  • Excellent order execution: Fidelity, unlike many online brokers, does not accept payment for order flow, and this shows in Fidelitys superior trade execution. 
  • Great screeners: Fidelity offers excellent, highly customizable stock, ETF, mutual fund, and fixed income screeners.
  • Free third-party research: Many brokers dont offer third-party research at all, but Fidelity offers it for free, allowing clients to get insights from professional analysts.

Cons Explained

  • Mobile app lacks punch for advanced users: While the mobile app is intuitive and easy to use, it does not have all the features available on Active Trader Pro. This means you cant enter conditional orders or enter multi-leg options positions on the mobile app. Advanced and high volume traders may want greater flexibility and control of their trading strategies.
  • No futures, options on futures, or spot forex: You cant trade spot foreign exchange, futures, or futures on options at Fidelity. If you want access to these markets, you will have to look elsewhere.
  • May not appeal to multi-leg options traders: Since you are paying for each leg of a trade, options trades at a seemingly reasonable rate of $0.65 per contract per turn can get expensive if entering into large multi-leg butterfly and condor options strategies.
  • Limited cryptocurrency options: Fidelity offers direct trading in Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Litecoin. While this is better than what many competitors offer, crypto-focused traders may have to look elsewhere.

Usability

Like most online brokers today, the account opening and setup process at Fidelity is quick and easy. The customer provides their name, address, and Social Security number, the broker quickly runs know-your-customer and money laundering checks, and assuming there are no problems, the account is open. There are no minimums required to open an account.

Fidelity offers multiple platforms, including a straightforward web-based platform best fit for beginner and intermediate traders. There is also a Trading Dashboard designed for intermediate and advanced users, but Fidelitys most powerful platform is a downloadable desktop program called Active Trader Pro, which offers various advanced trading tools and analytics.

Fidelity also has a well-rounded mobile app, although its functionality lags behind the Active Trader Pro platform. The app is fairly easy to navigate, and provides key information about a stock such as quotes, fundamental data like previous earnings, as well as analyst ratings. Placing orders on the mobile app is also intuitive and easy.

Trade Experience

It is easy to trade at Fidelity, regardless of your experience or trading style. The company has a web-based platform, a very powerful desktop trading application called Active Trader Pro, and mobile apps for iOS, Android, and Amazon devices.

In addition to basic trading functionality, there are plenty of other tools, including excellent screeners for stocks, ETFs, mutual funds, and options. All platforms support trading all the markets available, but Active Trader Pro is required for those looking to trade multi-leg options strategies, obtain streaming quotes, and enter conditional orders. Further, Active Trader Pro has superior charting abilities compared to other platforms.

Order execution at Fidelity is excellent, which is attributable to Fidelity not taking payment for order flow and the companys commitment to providing great execution. Fidelity also offers users research, including third-party research and Morningstar reviews, to help investors make investment decisions, along with news reports and all the tools, calculators, and analytical capabilities you could need.

Mobile Trade Experience

Mobile trading on Fidelity is good but fairly basic. Most advanced traders may find that Fidelitys mobile app is lacking the full functionality they need. There is no drawing on charts, and trading is limited, as conditional orders and multi-leg options strategies are not available on the mobile app. Further, the menu options are limited, with the choices of Investing, Transacting, Planning, and Discovery. The app also restricts order types to more basic ones, such as buy, sell, limit, stop, and trailing stop. The app works well and is straightforward to use, its just that it lacks more advanced functionality.

Trading Technology

Fidelity offers very solid trading technology. First off, the company does not accept payment for order flow, so it has excellent price execution. Fidelity reports price improvement on 95.47% of shares, an average execution speed of 0.04 seconds, and an effective price spread of $0.0031, resulting in average price improvement of $24.50 for a 1,000-share equity order, far above the industry average of $4.34.

The Active Trader Pro platform for the most advanced traders is incredibly customizable, allowing users to create a personal workstation with real time data, visual analytics, charting, and other trading tools.

In addition, Fidelity supports fractional share trading, dividend reinvestment, and the ability to control tax lots, and even offers paper trading through Active Trader Pro. Fidelity does not support trade journaling, but this can be integrated with third-party journaling platforms.

Costs

Much of Fidelitys offering is no-commission, and the few fees it charges are reasonable, which is why Fidelity is our current best broker for low costs. While Fidelitys charge of $0.65 per contract per round turn in options is not excessive, those trading multi-leg options strategies or are very active traders may prefer a lower-cost platform for options trading. Fidelitys commission schedule is transparent and easy to understand, with a breakdown as follows:

  • New issues: $0

  • No transaction fee (NTF) non-Fidelity funds: $0 on purchase; $49.95 redemption fee if the fund is held for less than 60 days

Bitcoin, Litecoin, and Ethereum

  • The trading fee is a 1% spread built into the price; no additional fees

Margin Rates

Fidelity utilizes a base rate and then adjusts the rate higher or lower depending on the assets in the account. The current base rate is 11.325%, and scales down as your account has more assets. The effective rate starts at 12.575% for accounts with debit balances less than $25,000, and at its lowest, is 8.25% for accounts with debit balances of $1 million or more. Although these margin rates are not out of line, and are even lower than some of Fidelitys competitors, they are not low. Very active traders utilizing margin extensively may consider a different broker, such as Interactive Brokers (IBKR), which charges just 5.83% for an account with $25,000, and as low as 5.17% for accounts with more than $3.5 million. As such, an account with $25,000 would have a margin rate difference of 624 basis points between the two brokers, potentially leading to significant expenses for frequent margin users.

How This Broker Makes Money From You and For You

Fidelity makes money from customers in multiple ways, but payment for order flow is not one of them, in contrast to many other online brokers. Further, Fidelitys fees are almost nonexistent. Fidelity does, however, generate income from its customers in multiple ways, including:

  • Margin interest: The difference between the margin rates it charges clients and the rate at which Fidelity can borrow funds can be as high as 600 basis points, generating income for the company.
  • Stock loans: Fidelity, with the account owners permission, will use the stock you own to lend out to people who short that security. Fidelity will split the income generated from stock lending with the customer.
  • Interest on money market funds: Fidelitys money market mutual funds pay interest at a rate lower than their borrowing rate, allowing the company to generate income from the cash in customer accounts. It is not uncommon for brokers to generate income from their customers accounts.
  • Wealth management and robo investing fees: Fidelity Wealth Management, where you work one-on-one with a financial advisor, is available for accounts with at least $500,000, while Fidelity Private Wealth Management is for accounts with more than $2 million and offers customers a wealth management team led by their financial advisor. Gross advisory fees ranging from 0.20% to 1.5% are applied to wealth management accounts, and a fee ranging from 0.20% to 1.04% is applied to assets in the private wealth management account. Robo investing, called Fidelity Go, has no fees on accounts with less than $25,000, and unlimited 1:1 coaching calls for a fee of 0.35% per year on balances above $25,000.

Fidelity makes money for you with:

  • Interest on cash balances: Fidelity currently pays 3.94% APY on uninvested cash held in client accounts.
  • Good price execution: Fidelity not taking payment for order flow results in savings to customers in the form of superior price execution.
  • Free trading for multiple markets: Fidelity customers have free trading for stocks, ETFs, and thousands of mutual funds.

Account and Research Amenities

Fidelity offers clients a great bang for their buck relative to creating tools, research, and other account amenities. Fidelity has excellent screeners for multiple markets, news, research, tools, calculators, idea generators, and educational content to help support its traders with the markets.

Stock Screener

Fidelity has an impressive stock screener that can be completely customized using over 140 criteria, both fundamental and technical. Strategies such as growth and value and other categories such as dividend payers, bargains, bottom-fishing, and more than two dozen criteria can be customized by the user. There are also pre-made screens that can be used immediately or customized further.

ETF Screener

Similar to the stock screener, Fidelity also offers a robust ETF screener, which allows users to search by fund family, market cap, sector, and other criteria. Further, there are an additional 86 criteria that can be customized, and include the ability to use technicals, fundamentals, investment philosophy, and anything else the user would want to filter on.

Mutual Fund Screener

Considering that Fidelity offers more than 10,000 mutual funds on its platform, its a good thing it has a good mutual fund evaluator/screener, which also includes preset screeners and the ability to customize based on categories and asset classes. Other criteria include Morningstar ratings, index funds, Fidelity funds, and environmental-social-governance (ESG), to name a few.

Options Screener

Fidelity offers an options strategy evaluator tool that allows you to compare a single-leg option or up to two multi-leg strategies on an underlying symbol. Results are paired with the strategy, along with a net price. Further, the results can be modeled to show expected outcomes based on your view of the market.

Crypto Screener

Fidelity does not offer a cryptocurrency screener, although it does offer news, quotes, and charts for cryptocurrencies.

Fixed Income Screener

Screeners for fixed income work a bit differently, in that there is a dedicated section for bonds, CDs, fixed income mutual funds, and ETFs. From this section, you can use the fixed income menu to choose what you are looking for. Listings show various credit qualities and maturities in a grid so you can find the security that best suits your needs. Dropdown menus in the fixed income section allow for further narrowing of the screen, such as looking for an interest payment of 5%.

Tools and Calculators

Fidelity is full of tools and calculators, and has a page that allows users to choose the tools they want to use in an intuitive way. There are more than 40 different tools available. You can find a tool by topic, description, or name, and even filter based on either product or topic, such as estate planning, charitable giving, retirement goals, investing strategies, and more. Users can also budget and see all of their holdings, including from outside accounts, and organize their finances with the Fidelity Full View tool.

Charting

Charts are available on all platforms. They are fairly basic on the web and mobile apps, and have the ability to change the time component of charts and add a series of indicators such as moving averages, Bollinger bands, and many others. Active Trader Pro, however, allows for further customization and drawing on charts with many indicators, making it the platform of choice for traders who require advanced technical analysis.

Trading Idea Generators

Trading ideas can be generated through the customizable tools on Active Trader Pro, which include a strategic idea tool, filters, and technical analysis. The key tools for generating ideas on Active Trader Pro include filters, real time analytics, and Trade Armor, which provides charting, news, and the ability to set exit strategies that utilize a visual trade ticket that the user can use to drag and drop stop order to support levels or limit orders to resistance points. The tickets created also show the profitability or potential loss, depending on which order is filled.

News

Fidelity offers customers helpful news and related information, such as economic release calendars. The primary news page is segmented into helpful sections, such as U.S. markets, company news, investing ideas, U.S. economy, technology, and international. Fidelity curates its news from multiple sources, and the newsfeed can be customized to filter by source. News sources on Fidelity include Reuters, Benzinga, Kiplinger, and MT.

Third-Party Research

Fidelity offers third-party research for free, which is not something many brokers boast. When researching a stock, users get to see an average rating and multiple research reports from multiple third-party researchers, including Zacks and Argus, to name a couple. Fidelitys free third-party research stands out compared to other online brokers.

Fractional Dividend Reinvestment Plan (DRIP)

Fidelity offers a dividend reinvestment program, where dividends received are automatically reinvested in the stock. The DRIP program can also be applied to fractionally purchased shares.

Cash Management

Fidelity has a cash sweep program where unused cash in an account is automatically swept into a Fidelity money market mutual fund. This provides interest income to the account owner. While the interest rate is not great on the FDIC-insured deposit Sweep Program mutual fund, with a 2.21% annual rate, it is still a better return than some of the other online brokers, some of which pay no interest on idle cash. The non-FDIC Insured sweep option is the Fidelity Government Money Market Fund, which currently has a 3.94% seven-day yield.

In addition to the cash sweep, Fidelity also offers other banking-related services, including credit cards, access to ATMs, and checking accounts.

Portfolio Analysis

Portfolio analysis is another strong area for Fidelity, as it has created a planning tool that customers can use on their own. You can also link external accounts to see your entire financial picture in one place. Analysis provided can show asset allocations, top holdings with their analyst ratings, and information on other asset types in your portfolio, such as fixed income. In addition, Fidelity Full View is another tool to help customers see their entire financial picture.

Education

Fidelity has a robust educational offering. There is a Learn tab at the bottom of the News & Research menu. Once in the Learn area, you can choose content based on topics such as financial essentials, life events, investing and trading, investment products, and advanced trading. There are also options to see the latest market insights, expert viewpoints, and money tips and tactics. There are articles, videos, webinars, and Q&A sessions for different topics. In addition to this educational content, Fidelity also provides a comprehensive glossary of investing terms.

Customer Service

Fidelity provides dedicated customer service, including phone and virtual assistant support 24/7 and live chat during normal business hours. Further, there is a help center and a YouTube channel that can answer many questions users may have about their account and Fidelitys services.

Security and Reliability

Fidelity is a secure platform, and the company has many protocols in place to identify and prevent suspicious activity, including multi-factor authentication with facial and fingerprint biometrics. Further, Fidelity will inform you of a login from a computer it does not recognize as your own. Fidelity also has a security center that allows account owners to manage their security settings, which can include granting access to third-party applications you use, setting up trusted devices, and disallowing the ability to transfer funds out of your account.

Fidelity also provides insurance protection that goes beyond the standard SIPC coverage.

Transparency

Fidelity is transparent. Its fees are easy to find and understand. Further, Fidelity voluntarily provides full price improvement information so clients can know what execution quality to expect.

The Bottom Line

There is a reason Fidelity is our current best overall broker, best broker for ETFs, and best broker for low costs: The company offers a powerful, comprehensive platform suitable for the needs of almost every trader and investor. It charges low to no fees while providing extensive account amenities such as news, charting, great research, and excellent tools like stock screeners, options analysis tools, and educational resources.

Users have the option to choose a platform that works best for them, from a basic web platform to the fully customizable Active Trader Pro. In addition, Fidelity charges very little in fees for services that other brokers charge for, and has offices all throughout the U.S. and the rest of the world to provide additional support. Fidelity simply covers all bases, and covers them well; it is a wise choice for any prospective client.
