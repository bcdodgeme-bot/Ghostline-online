Frequently asked questions


What should be included in a business letter?

A business letter should include your contact information, the date the letter was written, the recipient’s contact information, a salutation, a body, a closing salutation, and a signature (handwritten if printed or with an e-signature application if digital).

How should you structure a formal letter?

The body of a formal letter usually includes three parts:

Introduction:

State the purpose of your letter. Explain why you're writing it, state what you hope to achieve, and give an overview of the important information you’ll cover.

Main text:

Provide details about the important information you outlined in the introductory paragraph. This can be anywhere from one to five paragraphs.

Conclusion:

Summarize everything you’ve discussed. Include, if relevant, a call to action or next steps.

How should you address a letter?

To address a letter, start by putting your contact information (your name, job title, company, business address, business phone number, and business email address) left-aligned at the top. Then skip a line at the end of your information and insert the date you’re writing the letter. Then skip another line before adding your recipient’s contact information (their name, job title, company, and company address).

How should you start a letter?

Formal letters always have a greeting at the beginning of the written content as a cue that a message is about to begin. This is known as the salutation.

Most salutations begin with “Dear,” followed by the name of the recipient. All salutations use title capitalization and end in a comma.

If you don’t know the name of the receiver, you can also use the company name, the job title, or even the department name, for example, “Dear HR Representative.” As a last resort, you can use the generic salutation “To Whom It May Concern” in any circumstance. Try to avoid “Dear Sir or Madam,” as it’s a little outdated. And to avoid misgendering, refrain from adding a title like Mr., Ms., or Mx.

How should you end a letter?

Formal letters use a standard complimentary close or sign-off, similar to the salutation, before ending with an authentic signature.

One of the most common closes is “Sincerely,” including some variations like “With sincere gratitude,” or “Sincerely yours.” Other common sign-offs include “Best” and “Yours.” Unlike salutations, closes use sentence capitalization. Always capitalize the first letter of your complimentary close, but only the first letter. And just like the salutation, always end with a comma.

Below the closing, leave four to six lines blank so that you can sign the document either after you’ve printed it or via an e-signature application. After those four lines, type your full name and your title on the line below your name.

What information should I include in my prompt?

When using Grammarly’s AI assistance to help you create a formal letter, it’s best to provide a good amount of information. The more detailed your prompts are, the clearer and more personalized your business letter will be. To start, be sure to include the following information in your prompt:

  • Specific details about why you’re writing
  • The tone you’d like your professional letter to have
  • The information you’d like to include in the header and/or footer of your letter, such as your name and address, the date, etc.
