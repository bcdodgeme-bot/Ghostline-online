What is generative AI?

Generative AI is a type of artificial intelligence that creates new content, such as text, images, or audio, by analyzing vast amounts of data. When given specific prompts and guidelines, generative AI models can replicate tasks like composing an email, writing an article, generating ideas, and more.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is generative AI?

Generative AI is a type of artificial intelligence that creates new content—such as text, images, audio, and even code. Using deep learning architectures, it learns patterns in existing data to generate original outputs that resemble human-created writing, visual art, music, and more.

What is the difference between AI and generative AI?

Artificial intelligence (AI) is a broad field encompassing various technologies aimed at mimicking human intelligence, including problem-solving, decision-making, and understanding language.

Generative AI is a specialized subset of AI, particularly within machine learning (ML), that focuses on creating new content—such as text, images, or audio—by learning patterns from large datasets.

While traditional AI is often used to analyze data and make predictions, generative AI leverages deep learning to generate original outputs, rather than just interpreting existing data.

Is Grammarly considered generative AI?

Grammarly is an AI assistant that uses generative AI to help you write, rewrite, adjust your tone, reply to messages, brainstorm, and more. Generative AI is just one of many forms of AI that Grammarly uses to enhance communication and provide valuable writing suggestions.

Can Grammarly detect AI-generated content?

Grammarly offers Authorship, allowing you to track how you create text and prove where your work comes from. We also offer an AI detector, which identifies whether the text you provide resembles patterns found in AI-generated content.
