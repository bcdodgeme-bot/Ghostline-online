Planning for Responsible AI: Security and Transparency in Institution-Wide Adoption

Higher education institutions are embracing AI tools to enhance student learning and operational efficiency. However, scaling from departmental pilot programs to institution-wide adoption presents new security and transparency challenges. 

How can institutions build trust and facilitate transparent AI integration across the entire campus?

Watch this webinar to learn how Rutgers University is successfully navigating these challenges. Hear from experts Brian Luper, Director of Enterprise Service Delivery at Rutgers University’s Office of Information Technology, and Shevon Jackson, Senior Director of Information Technology at Rutgers Business School. They will share their journey of expanding Grammarly’s adoption from the Rutgers Business School to the entire institution and explain how they addressed security challenges while driving engagement and buy-in across the university.

This session offers:
  • Candid insights from Rutgers University’s IT leaders
  • Strategies for expanding AI adoption from departmental to institution-wide implementation
  • Approaches to addressing security concerns to build trust among stakeholders
  • Practical steps to engage key stakeholders and gain buy-in for AI initiatives

Meet Our Speakers

Cliff Archey

Lead PMM, Institutions and Students, Grammarly

Cliff Archey is the lead Product Marketing Manager for education institutions and students at Grammarly. Cliff has over a decade of experience in education as a teacher, college-to-career coach, and ed-tech product marketer in K-12 and higher ed.

Brian Luper

Director, Enterprise Service Delivery, Rutgers University

Brian Luper is an IT Director at Rutgers University, the State University of New Jersey. As part of the Office of Information Technology, he helps facilitate enterprise device management, departmental IT support, and software licensing. Additionally, Brian chairs the BTAA Endpoint Management Peer Group and serves as the EDUCAUSE IT Procurement and Supplier Management Community Group co-chair.

Shevon Jackson

Senior Director of IT, Rutgers Business School

Shevon Jackson is the Senior Director of Information Technology at the Rutgers Business School. His team supports classroom instruction & innovation, digital learning, CRM, and other enterprise applications along with emerging technologies throughout the Business School.
