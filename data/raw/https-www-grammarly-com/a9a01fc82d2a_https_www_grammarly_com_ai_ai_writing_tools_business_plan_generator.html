Frequently asked questions


What is a business plan?

A business plan is a document that articulates the strategic roadmap for a new business, outlining the company’s name, products or services, opportunities, goals, financial projections, target market, and mission statement.

What is the purpose of a business plan?

Business plans serve as your company’s North Star, helping to provide clear articulations of your business’s goals, financial projections, and more. Learning how to make a business plan serves as an important step in creating your company, as the document plays a big role in creating your business strategy and helping you secure funding. It’s important to ensure your business plan is polished, mistake-free, and written in a confident, formal tone that will resonate with potential investors.

What are the different types of business plans?

Business plans typically fall into three categories: one-page business plans, internal business plans, and traditional business plans. The one-page plan is a high-level summary of the business with key elements only, and is often used in a pitch capacity. Internal business plans are frequently used for starting up new arms of existing businesses. Traditional business plans are comprehensive documents, often dozens of pages in length, that forecast multiple years into the future and contain every detail that might contribute to the business’s success.

What info should be included in a business plan?

Your business plan should include the name of your business, along with what type of business it is, and the product or service you offer; your target market, including who they are, their needs, and how your business will reach them; your business’s goals and competitive advantage; and a detailed financial plan, including pricing strategy, overhead costs, timeline to profitability, current funding levels, and funding goals/needs.

What is an executive summary in a business plan?

A business plan’s executive summary is a high-level overview of your company’s path to succeed, complete with all the basic information about your business, financial projections, target market, and mission statement.

How should a business plan start?

A business plan should start with an introduction to the business and its offerings, and then convincingly demonstrate the benefits it provides to users and why it makes sense in the marketplace. If you’re writing a traditional, comprehensive business plan, it should start with a high-level executive summary.

If you're wondering how to write a business plan, Grammarly's AI-powered business plan generator is a great resource.

How long does it take to write a business plan?

How long it takes to write a business plan depends on what kind of business plan you’re writing. A one-page business plan can be written rather quickly (in seconds, if you’re using Grammarly's AI-powered business plan generator). Business plan length can increase substantially for those writing a traditional plan, which is typically a much longer and more comprehensive document that can span multiple, if not dozens of, pages.

What is a business plan generator?

A business plan generator is an AI-powered tool that uses a variety of prompts from the user to automatically generate a business plan. It is an extremely fast, effective tool that can help you compose a quality one-page business plan in seconds.

What information should I include in my prompt?

When using Grammarly’s AI assistance to help you create your business plan, it’s best to provide a good amount of information. The more detailed your prompts are, the clearer and more personalized your business plan will be. To start, be sure to include the following information in your prompt:

- The name of your business, along with what type of business it is and the product or service offered
- Your target market, including who they are, their needs, and how your business will reach them
- Your business’s goals, and the competitive advantage you hold in the market
- A financial plan, including pricing strategy, overhead costs, timeline to profitability, current funding, and funding goals or needs.
