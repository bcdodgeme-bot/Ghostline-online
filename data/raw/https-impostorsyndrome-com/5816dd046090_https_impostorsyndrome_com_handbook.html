The Impostor Syndrome Handbook for Coaches

Impostor Syndrome is Everywhere…
Yet Most People Have NO Idea What to Do About It

Millions worldwide secretly worry they’re not as competent, talented, or qualified as other people “think” they are — many of whom either are or will be your clients.  

This phenomenon has been the subject of countless articles, blogs, podcasts, and hundreds of academic studies. 

Although well-meaning, most of the posts, emails, and articles that claim to teach you to “cure” impostor syndrome are either unhelpful or potentially harmful. And that doesn’t sit well with us. 

In this guide, you’ll discover:

  • How to identify Impostor syndrome in your clients 
  • What NOT to do (HINT: the “pep talk” approach doesn’t work!)
  • How to see impostor syndrome for what it really is so that you can BEST help your clients…
  • And much more!

Impostor syndrome isn’t just an “interesting self-help topic.”

The stakes are high, and your clients need you NOW.

About Impostor Syndrome Institute™

Our mission is to address the (avoidable) impact of impostor syndrome on individuals and organizations through Humble Realist™ thinking. 

Impostor Syndrome Institute (ISI) is the official provider of information, insight and tools to organizations and individuals since 1983.

ISI was co-founded by global thought leader Dr. Valerie Young, widely recognized as the foremost expert on impostor syndrome, and Carolyn Herfurth, a leading business strategist with experience that cuts across Fortune 500s, mid-sized businesses and independents such as speakers, thought leaders and small business owners.

We help coaches, speakers, and organizations through our Impostor Syndrome Informed-Coach™ program, speaker licensing, facilitator training for organizations, and more. 

Our strong commitment to diversity, inclusion, and equity in all forms is woven throughout all of our solutions.
